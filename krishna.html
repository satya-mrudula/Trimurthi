Krishna

The Supreme Avatar of Lord Vishnu

The eighth avatar of Vishnu, the Preserver of the Universe — Krishna descended into the mortal world as a complete divine incarnation, Purna Avatar, embodying eternal love, transcendent wisdom, and the infinite play of cosmic consciousness.

The Descent

Birth of the Divine

Krishna was born on the eighth day of the dark half of the month of Bhadrapada (Krishnashtami) in Mathura to Princess Devaki and Vasudeva. His birth was heralded by celestial events — divine music, fragrant winds, and the blooming of lotus flowers at midnight.

Prophecy foretold that the eighth child of Devaki would slay the tyrant king Kamsa, her own brother. To protect the infant, Vasudeva carried the newborn Krishna across the flooded Yamuna river on a stormy night, guided by divine protection, to the village of Gokula, where he was raised by the cowherd Nanda and Yashoda.

His Essence

Divine Attributes

Krishna is Purna Avatar — the fullest, most complete descent of Vishnu — possessing all sixteen divine qualities (Shodasha Kala) simultaneously.

🦚
Peacock Feather

Adorned always on his crown, symbolising beauty, grace, and the cosmic dance of creation.

🎵
The Flute (Bansuri)

His enchanting music draws all creation toward the divine — the call of the soul back to God.

💙
Azure Form

His blue-dark complexion, like a rain cloud or the infinite sky, represents the limitless and all-pervasive divine.

🪷
Sudarshana Chakra

The spinning discus of divine justice — the cosmic wheel that destroys ignorance and evil.

⭐
Kaustubha Gem

The radiant jewel adorning his chest, representing the pure consciousness that illuminates all existence.

🌿
Vaijayanti Garland

A garland of five-coloured flowers, representing the five elements that constitute the cosmos.

The Sacred Story

The Divine Lilas

Krishna's life, from cradle to cosmic war, is a series of divine plays (lilas) that reveal the interplay of the human and divine.

Childhood · Gokula & Vrindavan

The Butter Thief of Vrindavan

As a child in Gokula and Vrindavan, Krishna charmed all with his mischievous ways — stealing butter, dancing with gopis, and playfully teasing his mother Yashoda. He killed the demoness Putana, lifted the mighty Govardhana Hill on his little finger to shield the villagers from Indra's wrath, and performed the Rasa Lila — the divine circular dance that is a metaphor for the soul's longing for union with the infinite.

Youth · Mathura

The Slaying of Kamsa

Summoned to Mathura by the wicked King Kamsa under the guise of a wrestling tournament, the young Krishna defeated the mightiest wrestlers and ultimately slew Kamsa himself, freeing his parents from captivity and fulfilling the divine prophecy that had been ordained before his birth.

Statecraft · Dwarka

The Architect of Dharma

Krishna established the magnificent city of Dwarka in the sea, becoming its king. He emerged as a supreme statesman, counsellor, and protector of righteousness. As a diplomat and ally, he worked tirelessly to prevent the catastrophic war of Kurukshetra — and when all efforts failed, he stood as the charioteer of the righteous Pandavas.

Kurukshetra · The Great War

The Bhagavad Gita

On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, when Arjuna collapsed in grief at the prospect of fighting his own kinsmen, Krishna revealed the eternal truths of life, duty, devotion, and liberation. This divine dialogue — the Bhagavad Gita — became the most celebrated scripture of the Hindu tradition, a timeless guide to righteous living for all of humanity.

✦ ✦ ✦

Whenever righteousness declines and unrighteousness rises, I manifest myself. For the protection of the good, for the destruction of evil, and for the re-establishment of virtue, I take birth age after age.

— Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 4, Verses 7–8

Bhagavad Gita

Krishna's Eternal Teachings

On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, Krishna imparted wisdom that transcends time — the Bhagavad Gita, the "Song of God." Its 700 verses contain the distilled essence of the Upanishads and the Vedic tradition.

01
Karma Yoga

Perform your duty without attachment to results. Act righteously and offer every action to the divine — this is the path of selfless action that liberates the soul.

02
Jnana Yoga

The Self (Atman) is eternal, indestructible, and beyond birth and death. True knowledge is the recognition that the individual soul is identical to the universal Brahman.

03
Bhakti Yoga

Surrender wholeheartedly to the divine with unwavering love and devotion. Of all yogis, the one whose inner Self is merged in Me with devotion is the most integrated with Me.

04
Dhyana Yoga

Through disciplined meditation, silence the restless mind and behold the light of the Self within. The yogi who has conquered the mind finds peace here and now.

05
Raja Yoga

Master the senses and mind. He who has no attachments, neither rejoicing in good fortune nor disturbed in bad, stands firm with steady wisdom.

06
Surrender (Prapatti)

Abandon all duties and take refuge in Me alone. I shall liberate you from all sins; do not grieve. This is the culminating teaching — total surrender as the highest path.

Eternal Influence

Legacy Across the Ages

Few figures in world history have inspired as vast a creative, spiritual, and philosophical heritage as Lord Krishna. Celebrated across every art form — from the classical sculptures of Khajuraho and Hampi, to the Manipuri and Bharatanatyam dance traditions, to the Bhakti poetry of Mirabai, Surdas, and Jayadeva's Gita Govinda — Krishna remains a living presence in the daily devotion of over a billion Hindus worldwide.

The Bhagavad Gita, his supreme discourse, has been translated into hundreds of languages and studied by luminaries from Mahatma Gandhi to Albert Einstein, from Emerson to Thoreau. ISKCON temples across 100 countries carry his name and teachings to every corner of the earth. The festival of Janmashtami — celebrating his birth — is observed with midnight vigils, fasting, and joy wherever Hindus gather.

As Vishnu's eighth and most complete avatar, Krishna is not a historical figure confined to the past — he is the living principle of divine love and wisdom, ever-present and ever-accessible to the sincere devotee.
