Vishnu
Narayana · Hari · The All-Pervading Preserver

Reclining in eternal serenity upon the thousand-hooded cosmic serpent Ananta Shesha, adrift on the primordial ocean of existence — Vishnu the all-pervading sustains every atom of creation through the infinite grace of his divine will. When Dharma falters, he descends.

His Divine Form

Sacred Attributes of Vishnu

Vishnu is depicted in resplendent blue — the colour of infinity itself — bearing four divine arms, each holding one of his sacred emblems, adorned with the Kaustubha gem and the Vaijayanti garland. He rides the mighty eagle Garuda across the heavens.

Divine Consort

Lakshmi — Goddess of Eternal Fortune

Lakshmi
Shri · Padmavathi · Goddess of Fortune & Divine Abundance · Beloved of Vishnu

Lakshmi — Sri herself, the radiant goddess of all abundance — arose from the churning of the cosmic ocean seated upon a fully-bloomed lotus, showered by celestial elephants, radiating golden light that made even the gods hold their breath. She is inseparable from Vishnu; wherever he is, she is present; without her, his preservation of the cosmos becomes impossible.

She manifests as Ashta Lakshmi — eight divine forms bestowing every form of true wealth: material abundance, grain, courage, knowledge, children, royal power, victory, and eternal spiritual fortune. She is the Shakti of Vishnu — the very energy through which the universe is sustained and blessed.

O Mahalakshmi, born of the ocean of milk, remover of all sorrow — the eternal mother of the three worlds who grants all boons to her devotees.— Mahalakshmi Ashtakam

The Ten Descents

Dashavatar — Avatars of Vishnu

Vishnu descends to earth in ten primary incarnations whenever the balance of cosmic order (Dharma) is imperilled — each avatar perfectly suited to the challenge of its age.

Aquatic Age
Matsya — The Fish

Vishnu took the form of a giant fish to rescue the Vedas from the cosmic flood and to save Manu, the progenitor of humanity, preserving the seeds of the next creation.

Cosmic Churning
Kurma — The Tortoise

As the cosmic tortoise, Vishnu bore Mount Mandara on his back during the churning of the ocean of milk, enabling gods and demons to produce Amrita, the nectar of immortality.

Retrieval of Earth
Varaha — The Boar

When the demon Hiranyaksha dragged the earth to the cosmic depths, Vishnu became a mighty boar and dove into the primordial waters to rescue and restore it upon his tusks.

Divine Justice
Narasimha — The Man-Lion

To defeat Hiranyakashipu — invulnerable to gods, men, or beasts, by day or night, inside or outside — Vishnu manifested as a being half-man, half-lion, at twilight, on the threshold.

Treta Yuga
Rama — The Ideal King

The embodiment of righteousness across every aspect of life, Rama defeated the demon king Ravana and established Ram Rajya — the golden age of divine governance on earth.

Dwapara Yuga
Krishna — The Supreme Avatar

The most complete descent — the divine flautist of Vrindavan, statesman of Dwarka, charioteer of Kurukshetra, and revealer of the eternal Bhagavad Gita.
